Our Foundation Story

Free the REAL FOUNDATION was forged in the fire of struggle and resolve. Our work began when Elijah Stroman walked out of prison after 24 years and found a world he could not recognize. The systems that decide so much of daily life — housing, work, banking, benefits, communication — had become driven by software, interfaces, and processes that Elijah had almost no opportunity to learn inside. He left prison with only the basic, brittle computer skills the system allowed, then stepped into a society run by programs he had never seen.

Less than six months later Elijah died. His final months were defined not only by the stigma of incarceration but by repeated, exhausting clashes with invisible technical barriers that blocked his path to stability. Those barriers cost him time, peace, dignity, and ultimately a life that deserved better. Elijah's experience became a call to action we could not ignore.

Our Mission

Free the REAL FOUNDATION provides structured, practical guidance that helps people who have been incarcerated a decade or longer reclaim their place in a digital society. We teach the technical fluency and systems navigation skills modern life demands so returning citizens do not have to trade dignity for survival.

Programs

We restore agency by translating opaque technologies into repeatable, teachable steps. Our curriculum and navigation services shorten the time it takes to secure housing, benefits, work, and medical care. We reduce frustration, lower barriers to opportunity, and help people move from crisis to stability.

The difference is decisive: a working email, a verified ID, a direct-deposit setup, or a completed benefits application can be the pivot between homelessness and housing, unemployment and a job, uncertainty and dignity.

About Elijah

Elijah Stroman spent 24 years incarcerated, using every possible moment to learn and grow within the strict limits of prison access. When he came home, the world had been reshaped by automated systems and digital gates he had not seen. The frustration and helplessness he felt while navigating those systems became the spark for Free the REAL FOUNDATION. Elijah's dignity, insight, and unfinished dream guide our work.

Programs & Resources

System Integration Curriculum

A repeatable, classroom-ready curriculum that breaks complex systems into small actionable steps. Modules include creating and protecting email, setting up direct deposit, applying for state IDs, understanding credit basics, navigating job portals, and using public benefits sites.

Hands-On Workshops

Low-stress, device-forward sessions that combine repetition, real interfaces, and patient instruction so learning sticks. Workshops are taught in community spaces, reentry centers, shelters, and partner sites.

One-on-One Navigation Support

Mentors and navigators sit side-by-side with participants to complete forms, calls, and verification flows until independence is achieved. This reduces trauma, speeds outcomes, and preserves dignity.

Community Partnerships

We partner with shelters, reentry programs, employers, and legal advocates to ensure training aligns with real-world barriers and opportunities and to place learners into stable pathways after training.

Potential Impact

Technology should expand opportunity, not gatekeep it. For people returning after long incarceration, the invisible walls of automated systems are often taller than prejudice or past records. By making systems legible and teaching durable skills, we reduce recidivism risk, shorten the time to economic stability, and save lives.

Every device donated, hour volunteered, or partnership formed multiplies impact: fewer missed benefits, faster job placements, improved housing retention, and stronger community safety nets.
